A legacy interface in a modern world

When I joined the team, the product line spanned 60+ SKUs across two heritage brands. The GUI had not been meaningfully updated since 2009 — a full decade of technical debt rendered in 8-bit graphics on embedded Linux displays.

Unified design language

Both brands needed to feel premium yet distinct. Denon targets modern audiophiles; Marantz serves heritage purists. The system had to flex without fracturing — shared components, differentiated personality.
