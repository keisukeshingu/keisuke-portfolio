Creative Technologist
San Francisco / Tokyo

"Every new medium deserves a new interface. I've spent 25 years designing the bridge between creative tools and emerging technology."

I was part of the core group who built the creative software that defined the Mac. Desktop publishing gave way to music production, music to immersive experience, and experience to the intelligent enterprise. Each leap was a chance to rethink what an interface could be. The last five years: bringing that instinct to enterprise at global scale — from premium consumer hardware to AI-native manufacturing systems.

A Life at the Frontier

Each era brought a new canvas.
I designed the tools.

From QuarkXPress to AI-native enterprise systems, my career traces the arc of creative technology itself. Every transition demanded new thinking — new interaction models, new design systems, new ways to bridge human intention and machine capability.

1999–2007
The Printed Word

QuarkXPress — Where Digital Publishing Was Born

I joined Quark when QuarkXPress held 95% of the global publishing market. Every magazine, newspaper, and book you read was likely laid out in our software. This was my education in designing tools for creative professionals — people who demand precision, speed, and reliability.

Working on the software that defined desktop publishing taught me that the best creative tools become invisible. The interface should never compete with the work being created.

2007–2012
Sound & Signal

From Print to Music — Designing Instruments for a New Generation

The transition from print to music software was my first paradigm shift. At Native Instruments (Traktor), I designed interfaces for professional DJs — tools where latency is measured in milliseconds and the interface is performed, not just used.

Then came Miselu C.24 — a piano keyboard designed for the iPad generation. I hold the USPTO patent for this instrument, which was demonstrated at Google I/O. The belief: creative tools should be as portable as ideas.

2012–2016
The Live Stage

When Music Became Architecture

At Beatport and through festival collaborations, I moved from screen-based interfaces into spatial experience design. EDC, Ultra, Burning Man — these weren't just events, they were environments where sound, light, and interaction merged into architecture.

This era taught me to design for the body, not just the eye. When your canvas is a festival stage and your users are thousands of people moving through space, traditional UI thinking breaks down entirely.

2017–2021
Enterprise Hardware

Denon / Marantz — Premium Audio Meets Global Scale

As Director of UX, I led the transformation of two heritage audio brands across 60+ products. The challenge: migrate from 8-bit bitmap displays to modern vector-based interfaces while maintaining continuous product shipments across four global offices.

Key achievements: #5 to #1 market position, 50+ Apple-certified products, an industry-first embedded font licensing model with Monotype (later adopted by Audi, Porsche, BMW, and Mercedes), and a 35% reduction in customer support calls.

Marantz Cinema porthole / Denon receiver / unified product ecosystem
2023–Now
Intelligent Industry

AI-Native Enterprise — Where Human and Machine Collaborate

At Fabrion, I'm the founding designer of an AI-native manufacturing execution system where multi-agent AI and factory teams collaborate in real time. The design challenge: earning human trust in autonomous decision-making at industrial scale.

Simultaneously at TDK SensEI, I'm designing the dashboard that turns raw edge sensor data into predictive foresight for industrial B2B clients — unifying three merged enterprise brands into one coherent intelligence platform.
