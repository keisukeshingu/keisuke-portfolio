01 / 05

Situation

In 2017, Denon and Marantz sat at #5 in the premium home audio market. The products were acclaimed for sound quality — audiophiles trusted the engineering. But the user experience told a different story with product reviews averaging 3.5 stars.

The company faced a strategic misalignment: leadership wanted to compete with Sonos, Apple, Google, and Amazon in smart home and wireless streaming through the HEOS multi-room audio platform. But HEOS was not fully integrated with the core audio products.

The embedded display system across products was a relic — the TV GUI had the visual quality of an 8-bit Nintendo game. The underlying architecture used bitmap graphics rendering with no structured file system. Every string of text and icon was a rasterized image baked at a fixed resolution.

This approach created three cascading failures: resolution ceiling as the industry moved toward 4K/8K, localization paralysis requiring separate bitmaps per language, and consistency impossibility across 60+ SKUs.

02 / 05

Task

Unify and modernize the user experience across two premium audio brands and 60+ products — bridging embedded hardware, mobile software, TV output, and voice-assistant ecosystems — to make Denon/Marantz competitive with software-first companies like Sonos.

Success criteria included moving product reviews from 3.5★ to 4.0★+, reducing UX-related support calls, achieving AirPlay 2 and HomeKit certification across the product line, and establishing ecosystem coherence across all touchpoints.

Key constraints included extreme hardware limitations (1-3 inch displays), legacy platform migration without disrupting the active product pipeline, 60+ SKU breadth across two distinct brands, Apple certification requirements, and multi-platform delivery across embedded Linux, iOS, Android, and TV GUI.

03 / 05

Action

Research began with support data analysis — customer support call logs revealed UX-related calls were concentrated on setup and initial configuration. Product review mining showed 4.5-5.0★ sound quality scores but 3.5★ overall, confirming the UX gap.

Competitive benchmarking studied setup and daily-use flows of Sonos, Apple HomePod, Google Home, Amazon Echo, and Yamaha MusicCast. The benchmark was software-first companies resetting user expectations, not other AV receiver brands.

The foundational design decision was replacing the entire bitmap-based rendering system with Qt framework on embedded Linux, converting all graphics to SVG with font-embedding and database-driven content management. This enabled resolution independence, localization at scale, and systematic visual consistency.

I became the point person negotiating with Monotype to establish an embedded device font licensing framework covering per-device pricing at 60+ SKU scale, CJK character sets, embedded ROM constraints, and firmware update distribution. The resulting model saved multi-million dollars and was subsequently adopted by Audi, Porsche, BMW, and Mercedes.

For constrained 1-3 inch displays, I developed a typographic system optimized for small-screen legibility with custom font selection prioritizing x-height and stroke consistency. Navigation relied on physical buttons and knobs, not touch.

Created comprehensive HEOS Ecosystem Design Guidelines covering brand design language for both Denon (modern, approachable) and Marantz (premium, refined), cross-platform typography scales, iconography library in SVG, and multi-language specification with CJK support.

Redesigned the setup flow around three principles: guided onboarding via the companion app, multi-assistant integration for Siri/Alexa/Google Assistant, and progressive complexity keeping simple tasks simple.

Ensured AirPlay 2 and HomeKit certification for 50+ products by translating Apple HIG requirements into our design guidelines and creating per-category compliance checklists.

04 / 05

Results

Market position shifted from #5 to #1, with UX as the primary driver of category leadership. Product reviews improved from 3.5★ to 4.5★ average — a full star improvement.

Support calls decreased by 35%, driven by setup flow redesign, UI clarity improvements, and multi-platform consistency. AirPlay 2 certified products grew from 0 to 50+ — the largest portfolio in premium audio.

Delivered complete platform migration from bitmap to vector rendering (SVG + font-embedding + database-driven) on Qt/QML, from 480p to 1080p/8K-ready. Created distinct visual languages for both Marantz (teal HD GUI) and Denon (clean, modern interface).

The embedded font licensing model with Monotype became an industry standard, subsequently adopted across the automotive sector by Audi, Porsche, BMW, and Mercedes for their infotainment systems.

05 / 05

Design Thinking

My approach was to fix the underlying technical and design frameworks so that better UX became the default output. When the rendering system makes premium typography impossible, you replace the rendering system. When localization requires thousands of hand-crafted assets, you build database-driven content management.

Constrained hardware forces design discipline that unconstrained environments never demand. On a 1-3 inch display with physical buttons, every decision must answer: what does the user need to know right now, and how do I show it in the fewest pixels?

Three capabilities demonstrated here scale to any product design challenge: platform thinking over feature thinking (designing for 60+ SKUs forces systems thinking), hardware-software bridge (designing across embedded firmware, mobile apps, and TV interfaces), and measurable business impact through design (#5 to #1 market position).
