01 — Context

The Conference That Needed to Look Like Its Own Future

TechCrunch Disrupt is the flagship event for the global startup and venture capital ecosystem — three days at Moscone Center in San Francisco where product launches, billion-dollar funding announcements, and Startup Battlefield finals all compete for the same audience attention. In 2016, Keisuke Shingu and Kyle Gordon of Studio ZIRO were brought in to create the visual identity that would run across every stage, every keynote, and every branded moment throughout the event. The brief was simple and almost impossible: give this conference a visual system that looks as forward-thinking as the companies on stage.

02 — Concept

San Francisco as Data

The visual concept grew from a question: what does the city itself look like when you strip it down to pure information? Using real geographic map data and height-map elevation models of San Francisco, Keisuke and Kyle built a physically accurate, minimalist 3D rendering of the city — every block, every gradient of elevation from the waterfront to Twin Peaks, rendered as clean wireframe geometry.

Multiple camera animations were developed, each exploring different districts and sightlines through the model — from an aerial pull-out over the Embarcadero to tight street-level tracking shots through SoMa. The city became a living, abstract landscape that could be animated, lit, and branded without losing its geographic specificity. TechCrunch's visual identity was fused into the renders: color grading, type treatments, and motion pacing tuned to conference energy.

03 — Execution

Three Days, Every Screen

The final visual system ran across every touchpoint of the three-day event: opening sequences for keynote presentations, branded holding content between speaker transitions, stage backgrounds, and ambient screen content throughout the Moscone Center. The 3D city system was designed to be modular — different camera cuts and timing variations could be assembled for different session types without requiring re-renders, giving the production team flexibility while maintaining visual consistency across the full run of the conference.

Keynote Motion System · Stage Screens · Session Branding

"A conference visual system works when it disappears — when the audience stops noticing the screen and starts focusing on the idea. The city render was the stage. The speakers were the story."

04 — Thread

Visual Language for Technology Culture

TechCrunch Disrupt sits at the intersection of design, journalism, and startup culture — an audience that immediately reads visual quality as a signal. A conference that looks generic signals that the ideas inside are generic too. Building a visual system from real geographic data rather than stock motion graphics was both a practical and a philosophical choice: it meant the visuals were specific to this city, this event, this moment in 2016. The same principle connects to every other project in this portfolio — whether it is building a virtual world from Gundam's 45-year mythology or designing a Kunichika archive from the logic of the Ohe Collection. The data that already exists is always more interesting than the data you invent.
