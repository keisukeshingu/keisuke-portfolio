Festival & Conference Visuals

Real-Time Visuals at Scale

Producing real-time generative visuals for some of the world's largest music festivals and technology conferences — designing emotional experiences for audiences of 100,000+ through live rendering, projection mapping, and immersive installations.

Festival Visuals · EDC · Ultra · Detroit Movement

Role

Real-Time Visual Artist / Producer

Timeline

Platform

Generative visuals, LED stages, projection mapping

EDC Ultra Music Festival Detroit Movement Burning Man TechCrunch Disrupt Generative Art Projection Mapping

I produced real-time generative visuals for main stages and art installations at Electric Daisy Carnival (100,000+ attendees), Ultra Music Festival (50,000+ per day with international broadcast), Detroit Movement (the birthplace of techno), Burning Man (immersive participatory installations in extreme conditions), and TechCrunch Disrupt (bridging tech and creative worlds).

Producing real-time visuals for 100,000 people teaches design lessons that no screen-based practice can. Zero-latency feedback loops — when visuals synchronize with live music, any latency is immediately visible and felt. This demanded mastery of real-time rendering pipelines and performance optimization — the same discipline required for streaming AI interfaces and real-time data visualization.

Main Stage · Generative Visuals Synchronized with Performance

Emotional design at scale — a festival stage visual isn't a UI, it's an emotional architecture. You're designing how 100,000 people feel simultaneously. System reliability under pressure — there are no bug fixes at a live festival. The system works or it doesn't, in front of an enormous audience.

Hardware-software-human integration — festival visuals respond to the DJ, the music, the crowd energy, and the physical space simultaneously. This multi-input, real-time, human-in-the-loop design thinking directly parallels the AI agent interfaces I design today. These skills are directly relevant to spatial computing platforms at Apple (Vision Pro), Meta (Quest), and Google — where physical and digital design merge.
