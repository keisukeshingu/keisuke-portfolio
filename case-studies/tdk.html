TDK SensEI

Three Companies, One Intelligent Interface

Designing the user experience for TDK's edge AI sensor platform — unifying incompatible metadata, sensor data models, and ML capabilities from three merged companies into a single coherent interface for industrial predictive maintenance.

TDK Corporation launched SensEI — a corporate venture consolidating edge AI capabilities from multiple acquisitions including InvenSense ($1.3B), Qeexo, Micronas, and Chirp Microsystems. The technical assets were world-class. The UX problem: three distinct companies had merged under one AI initiative, and nothing matched — metadata schemas, sensor data formats, ML model outputs all incompatible.

The flagship product edgeRX needed to present itself as a unified machine health monitoring platform. I joined as UX strategy consultant to define the experience. The design couldn't wait for complete backend unification — that would take years. I designed a presentation layer that normalized the user experience while underlying data architecture was being harmonized.

Platform Partner Ecosystem

I created a layered abstraction model: a single 0-100 health score for plant managers, per-sensor summary cards with sparkline trends for reliability engineers, and full domain-specific visualization (FFT spectra, waveforms, time-series) for specialists. This progressive disclosure serves all three user types without forcing anyone into unnecessary complexity.

The biggest UX failure in predictive maintenance systems is showing that something might go wrong without telling users what to do. I designed actionable prediction cards with four required elements: what's happening (plain language), when it matters (predicted timeline), what to do (specific maintenance action), and business impact (cost of action vs. inaction).

Rather than forcing immediate data standardization, the UX normalization layer unified alert taxonomy to a consistent 4-level severity system, standardized confidence display across all ML model outputs, and created cross-sensor correlation views that showed three separate alerts from three systems as one correlated insight.

The UX normalization layer can ship independently of data standardization — making the product feel unified from Day 1.
