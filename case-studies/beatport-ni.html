The Platform

Beatport is the definitive distribution platform for electronic music, where every major and independent label needed to exist in the professional DJ ecosystem. At its peak, Beatport wasn't competing for the market — it was the market.

The critical challenge was the supply side: the infrastructure that powers what the storefront shows. You can't sell what isn't uploaded correctly, can't surface releases without proper metadata, and can't build a market with broken label tools.

The Label Portal

I led product on Beatport's B2B label portal — the CMS that labels use to upload audio, set pricing, manage metadata, and track catalogue performance. Major labels like Toolroom, Defected, and Bedrock, along with thousands of independent operations, managed their entire supply relationship through this system.

Supply-side product challenges differ fundamentally from consumer UX: labels need high-volume batch operations, complex metadata, audio validation, and business-critical release timing. The portal had to serve both sophisticated major-label teams and solo operators working from laptops.

Platform Strategy

Platform strategy meant expanding from the supply side: increasing content volume and quality, bringing in new label categories, and making Beatport the priority in distribution decisions. Tool structure shapes user behavior and downstream market outcomes.

Every decision about the portal cascades through the ecosystem: affecting what gets distributed, how it's priced, what audiences discover, and what DJs play. Understanding these feedback loops became my core lens for platform design.

Beatport is where DJs buy music. I built the other side — the system that gets it there.

Native Instruments → Traktor

Beatport and Native Instruments were deeply intertwined — Beatport was the distribution, Traktor was the performance software. Moving to Native Instruments as Product Manager completed the loop from distribution infrastructure to live performance software.

Traktor is professional DJ software requiring fluency in audio architecture and professional DJ workflows: cueing, transitions, waveform displays, and controller mappings. I'd been running real-time audio-visual systems at festivals while working on this — the two were never separate.
