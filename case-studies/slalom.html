Gundam Metaverse · Slalom Consulting

Designing the World Fans Step Into

Bandai Namco invested $130M to build SIDE-G — a virtual space where 45 years of Gundam IP becomes a world fans inhabit, not just consume. As Director of Experience Design at Slalom Consulting Tokyo, I led UX strategy for how fans discover, navigate, and own this world.

When a beloved IP becomes a place — not a show, not a game, but a world fans inhabit — the design questions change fundamentally. Navigation isn't a menu, it's spatial wayfinding. Character encounters aren't cutscenes, they're fan rituals. The challenge Bandai Namco brought to Slalom was this: how do you translate 45 years of Gundam lore into an experience architecture that feels native to the fans who grew up inside it?

SIDE-G is Gundam Metaverse's answer — a series of virtual space colonies, each mapped to a distinct era of the franchise. My role was defining the UX strategy that makes a fan feel like a pilot, not a visitor. That meant working from the inside out: starting with fan mental models of the Gundam universe — the federations, the colony clusters, the allegiances — and building the world's information architecture around those existing loyalties, not around generic virtual world conventions.

Avatar System · Fan Identity & Progression Architecture

The avatar system was designed as an identity object, not a customization feature. In Gundam culture, your mobile suit is an extension of your philosophy. We translated this into a progression model where fans earn affiliation markers, suit insignia, and colony access rights through engagement — not purchase. The journey from visitor to pilot to citizen mirrors the hero arc Gundam stories have always told.

An IP-native virtual world doesn't start with technology — it starts with the mythology fans already carry. Design your world to honour what they already believe.

Cross-media continuity was the hardest UX problem. SIDE-G had to coexist with 45 years of anime, manga, Gunpla kits, games, and concerts — without contradicting any of it. We designed entry points keyed to different fan types: the lore completionist, the builder, the social fan, the newcomer. Each path leads to the same world but through a different gate, preserving the depth of the IP while removing barriers to entry.

The Slalom engagement also encompassed innovation strategy across other frontier projects — Toyota's WOVEN City smart city ecosystem and partnerships with NVIDIA on real-time rendering infrastructure — giving this work additional technical depth and scale context.
