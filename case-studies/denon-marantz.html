Context

Two Heritage Brands, One Broken Experience

In 2017, Denon and Marantz sat at #5 in the premium home audio market. The products were acclaimed for sound quality — audiophiles trusted the engineering. But product reviews averaged 3.5 stars. The gap between sonic excellence and user experience was costing market position.

The embedded display system across products was a relic — the TV GUI had the visual quality of an 8-bit Nintendo game. Bitmap graphics rendering with no structured file system. Every string of text and icon was a rasterized image baked at a fixed resolution. This created three cascading failures: resolution ceiling as the industry moved toward 4K/8K, localization paralysis requiring separate bitmaps per language, and consistency impossibility across 60+ products.

Before — 480p Bitmap TV GUI Overlay on Premium Hardware

Meanwhile, leadership wanted to compete with Sonos, Apple, Google, and Amazon in smart home and wireless streaming through the HEOS multi-room audio platform. But HEOS was not fully integrated with the core audio products. The competitive benchmark wasn't other AV receiver brands — it was software-first companies resetting user expectations entirely.

The challenge: unify and modernize the user experience across two premium brands and 60+ products — bridging embedded hardware, mobile software, TV output, and voice-assistant ecosystems — to make Denon/Marantz competitive with software-first companies like Sonos.

Key constraints included extreme hardware limitations (1-3 inch displays with physical buttons, not touch), legacy platform migration without disrupting the active product pipeline, Apple certification requirements for AirPlay 2 and HomeKit, and multi-platform delivery across embedded Linux, iOS, Android, and TV GUI.

The Work

From Bitmap to Vector, From Confusion to Clarity

Research began with support data analysis — customer support call logs revealed UX-related calls were concentrated on setup and initial configuration. Product review mining confirmed the pattern: 4.5-5.0★ sound quality but 3.5★ overall. The UX gap was measurable and specific.

The foundational design decision was replacing the entire bitmap-based rendering system with Qt framework on embedded Linux, converting all graphics to SVG with font-embedding and database-driven content management. This wasn't a visual refresh — it was a platform migration that enabled resolution independence, localization at scale, and systematic visual consistency.

Before — Bitmap rendering, 480p, per-language image files
After — Vector SVG on Qt/QML, resolution-independent, database-driven

I became the point person negotiating with Monotype to establish an embedded device font licensing framework. The deal covered per-device pricing at 60+ product scale, CJK character sets, embedded ROM constraints, and firmware update distribution. The resulting model saved multi-million dollars and was subsequently adopted by Audi, Porsche, BMW, and Mercedes for their infotainment systems.

For the constrained 1-3 inch hardware displays, I developed a typographic system optimized for small-screen legibility with custom font selection prioritizing x-height and stroke consistency. Navigation relied on physical buttons and knobs — not touch — requiring a fundamentally different interaction model than mobile apps.

Constrained Display Typography · 1–3 Inch Embedded Screens

Created comprehensive HEOS Ecosystem Design Guidelines covering brand design language for both Denon (modern, approachable) and Marantz (premium, refined), cross-platform typography scales, iconography library in SVG, and multi-language specification with CJK support.

Redesigned the setup flow around three principles: guided onboarding via the companion app, multi-assistant integration for Siri/Alexa/Google Assistant, and progressive complexity keeping simple tasks simple.

Denon — Modern, approachable design language
Marantz — Premium, refined design language (teal HD GUI)

Ensured AirPlay 2 and HomeKit certification for 50+ products by translating Apple HIG requirements into our design guidelines and creating per-category compliance checklists. This made Denon/Marantz the largest Apple-certified portfolio in premium audio.

Impact

From #5 to #1

Market position shifted from #5 to #1, with UX as the primary driver of category leadership. Product reviews improved from 3.5★ to 4.5★ average — a full star improvement driven by setup flow redesign, UI clarity, and multi-platform consistency.

Support calls decreased by 35%, concentrated in the setup and configuration categories that the research had identified. AirPlay 2 certified products grew from 0 to 50+ — the largest portfolio in premium audio.

Before

480p overlay, pixelated bitmap rendering, per-language image files, no scalability

After

1080p/8K-ready vector rendering, font-embedded SVG, database-driven localization

Before

Zero Apple ecosystem integration, no voice assistant support

After

50+ AirPlay 2 / HomeKit products, Siri / Alexa / Google Assistant

The embedded font licensing model with Monotype became an industry standard, subsequently adopted across the automotive sector. The design system I established continues to ship across new Denon and Marantz products.

Reflection

Fixing the System, Not the Symptoms

My approach was to fix the underlying technical and design frameworks so that better UX became the default output. When the rendering system makes premium typography impossible, you replace the rendering system. When localization requires thousands of hand-crafted assets, you build database-driven content management.

On a 1-3 inch display with physical buttons, every decision must answer: what does the user need to know right now, and how do I show it in the fewest pixels?

Constrained hardware forces design discipline that unconstrained environments never demand. This project demonstrated three capabilities that scale to any product design challenge: platform thinking over feature thinking, the hardware-software bridge across embedded firmware and mobile apps, and measurable business impact through design.
