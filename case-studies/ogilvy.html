Context

Launch Audi's Electric Future in Japan

In November 2022, Audi Japan launched the Q4 e-tron — its first compact electric SUV for the Japanese market. Audi needed more than a press event. They needed an experience communicating four brand pillars — Design, Sustainability, Digitalization, and Performance — in a way that was felt, not just heard. The venue: Shin-Toyosu Brillia Running Stadium in Tokyo.

For Geometry Ogilvy Japan, this was the first project with Audi Japan. The relationship had no track record. Every decision would determine whether the agency won long-term trust. As Head of Technology, I was responsible for the full experience design — bridging creative vision with technical execution.

The immersive experience needed to be flawless — not just creatively ambitious, but technically reliable in a live event environment where failure is public and immediate.

The challenge was orchestrating four chapters across a 2000-person venue with zero technical failures. Spatial audio needed to reinforce multiple narratives simultaneously. Generative systems required real-time responsiveness with predictable behavior. AR performance demanded synchronized choreography tracking. Each system — generative, audio, AR, interactive — had to work independently and in concert.

The Work

Four Chapters of Progress

Chapter 1 — Design: "Creating Progress" featured an interactive art installation with four commissioned artists (WOK22, LUISE ONO, KAREZMAD, Tadaomi Shibuya). A real-time generative system blended attendee inputs with artist templates, ensuring every visitor's creation was unique. No two outputs identical. The installation became a participatory statement: design is responsive to human interaction.

Chapter 2 — Sustainability: "Sustaining Progress" displayed art installations constructed from recycled materials used in the vehicle's construction. The spatial audio environment reinforced the sustainability narrative — sound moving through space, reflecting the journey from material waste to automotive components. Attendees experienced sustainability not as claim but as material reality.

Chapter 3 — Digitalization: "Exciting Progress" featured an AR performance with actor-dancer Moriyama Mirai. Real-time AR content synchronized with live choreography, creating a hybrid human-digital presence. Spatial audio moved with the performer, creating three-dimensional narrative space. The performer became a bridge between digital capability and human emotion.

Chapter 4 — Performance: The test drive program. After three chapters of sensory engagement, attendees experienced the product itself. The narrative arc was deliberate: curiosity → understanding → wonder → ownership. You don't pitch performance; you let people feel it.

AI-Assisted Campaign Concepting & Creative System

The technical architecture coordinated five parallel systems: generative visuals (zero-latency audience interaction), spatial audio (multi-channel alignment with visuals), AR performance (camera tracking synchronized with live performer), interactive installations (sensor-based for non-technical attendees), and projection mapping (stadium-scale multi-projector coordination). Each system had independent failsafe logic. If generative visuals failed, installations continued. If AR tracking failed, spatial audio carried narrative. No single point of failure.

Reliability required redundant hardware, predictive load testing, and live system monitoring. Every component was stress-tested at 300% expected load. Network bandwidth was measured to the millisecond. Spatial audio channels were individually verifiable. The production team had practiced handoff sequences 50+ times before November 17.

Impact

Agency of Record

The four-chapter immersive launch executed on November 17, 2022 with zero technical failures. All real-time systems performed flawlessly. Attendees reported the experience as "overwhelming in the best way" — sensory engagement that created emotional ownership of the product story.

The direct outcome: Ogilvy was appointed Audi Japan's Brand & Creative Agency of Record in January 2023. This wasn't incremental — it was category leadership recognition. Press coverage across Car Watch, CAR and DRIVER Japan, EVsmart, and Yahoo News Japan amplified the event's impact beyond attendees.

Reflection

Experiences as Narrative Architecture

Every experience tells a story. The four-chapter structure wasn't arbitrary — it was a deliberate narrative arc grounded in how human attention and learning actually work. You don't use AR for sustainability messaging; you use physical materials. You don't use static displays for technological ambition; you use live performance. Medium and message must align.

From festival stages to brand experiences to product interfaces — the throughline is real-time, human-centered systems design at scale.

The skills from immersive experience design transfer directly to digital product work. Spatial audio principles become audio UX. Generative systems become dynamic UI. Audience flow patterns become user journeys. Live reliability requirements become production-grade software standards. Multi-sensory engagement becomes Vision Pro / Quest / ambient computing thinking.

This project demonstrated that the distinction between "physical" and "digital" is artificial. Immersive experiences are multi-platform systems. They require the same rigor as software — version control, load testing, monitoring, incident response. They require the same empathy as design — understanding how people actually perceive, move, and decide.
