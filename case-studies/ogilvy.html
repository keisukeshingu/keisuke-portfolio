Context

Launch Audi's Electric Future in Japan

In November 2022, Audi Japan launched the Q4 e-tron at Shin-Toyosu Brillia Running Stadium in Tokyo. The challenge was creating an immersive four-chapter experience communicating Design, Sustainability, Digitalization, and Performance to 2000 attendees with zero technical failures.

As Head of Technology at Geometry Ogilvy Japan, I orchestrated five parallel systems — generative visuals, spatial audio, AR performance, interactive installations, and projection mapping — each with independent failsafe logic.

The Work

Four Chapters of Progress

Chapter 1 — "Creating Progress": Real-time generative system with four commissioned artists created unique interactive outputs for each visitor.

Chapter 2 — "Sustaining Progress": Recycled materials installations with spatial audio reinforced sustainability narrative.

Chapter 3 — "Exciting Progress": Live AR performance with actor-dancer Moriyama Mirai synchronized to real-time choreography. Chapter 4 — Performance: Test drive experience completed the narrative arc.

AI-Assisted Campaign Concepting & Creative System

Technical reliability required redundant hardware, stress-testing at 300% load, and 50+ practice handoff sequences. Each system had independent failsafe logic — if one failed, others carried narrative continuity.

Impact

Agency of Record

The launch executed November 17, 2022 with zero technical failures. Ogilvy was appointed Audi Japan's Brand & Creative Agency of Record in January 2023, with coverage in Car Watch, CAR and DRIVER Japan, and EVsmart.

Reflection

Experiences as Narrative Architecture

The four-chapter structure grounded narrative arc in how human attention works: medium and message must align. Immersive experiences are multi-platform systems requiring the same rigor as software — load testing, monitoring, reliability — and the same empathy as design — understanding perception, motion, and decision-making.
